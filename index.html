§ 01 / THE CLAIM

Story is load-bearing
civilizational infrastructure.

We are making a livingry.

Storylivingry is a studio. We produce paradigm shifts through the synergy of films, technology, theatrical work, publications, governance frames, businesses, coalitions, economics — and the ontological scaffolding that holds them together.

"Production studio" undersells it. Story is the means by which humans coordinate, govern, evolve, and become themselves. When story is treated as such — as livingry, in Buckminster Fuller's term, the opposite of weaponry — then a studio that takes story seriously is doing something closer to civic architecture than to media production.

We don't fix the broken model. We build the alternative so well it makes the old one obsolete.
